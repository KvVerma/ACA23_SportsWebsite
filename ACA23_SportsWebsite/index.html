A tribute to the Best Swimmer in the World

Michael Phelps

Biography

Michael Phelps was born in Baltimore, Maryland and raised in Towson. He began swimming at the age of seven. When he was in the sixth grade, he was diagnosed with ADHD (Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder). By the age of 10, he held a national record in his age group for the 100-meter butterfly.

Phelps qualified for the 2000 Summer Olympics at the age of 15. He made the finals and finished fifth in the 200-meter butterfly.

Phelps broke the world record for the 200-meter butterfly at the World CHampionship Trials for the 2001 World Aquatic Championships, becoming the youngest male ever to set a world record in swimming, at the age of 15 years and 9 months.

Phelps then went on to win many more medals and securing many victories at the Olympics and other Championships He went on to win eight gold medals at the 2008 Beijing Games, break Mark Spitz's 1972 record of seven first-place finishes at any single Olympic Games, become the Most Successful athlete of the Olympic Games four times in a row, earned World Swimmer of the Year Award eight times, and countelss other achivements.

He started the Michael Phelps Foundation, which focuses on swimming and healthier lifestyles; was chosen to be the flag-bearer of the United States at the 2016 Summer Olympics Parade of Nations, and has won 82 medals in total, including 65 gold, 14 silver, and 3 bronze.

He retired on August 12, 2016 and is widely regarded as the best swimmer of all time and is also often often considered to be one of the greatest atheles of all time.
